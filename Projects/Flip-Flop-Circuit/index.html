Flip Flop Circuit

      What is a Flip Flop Circuit?

      A flip flop is a circuit that can hold a state after a short pulse sets it.

      While that might not make sense for some, the video will helpfully explain it.

      Looking at just one of the leds, lets say the top one, notice how it can be turned on indefinitly with a short click of a button. This is called "setting" the flip flop.

      If we want to turn the top one off, we can click the other button. This is called "reseting" the flip flop.

      Why 2 LEDS?

      The reason for two leds is to represent the two outputs of the flip flop which are often represented with Q and Q (Q not). Q will always be the oppisite of Q.
